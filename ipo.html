IPO
IPO stands for Initial Public Offering. It is a crucial event in the life of a company when it decides to go public and offer its shares to the general public for the first time on a stock exchange. During an IPO, the company raises capital by selling ownership stakes (shares) to investors, thereby becoming a publicly traded entity. This process involves thorough regulatory compliance, financial disclosures, and underwriting by investment banks. IPOs provide an opportunity for companies to access a broader pool of capital, increase visibility, and facilitate future growth, while investors can potentially benefit from capital appreciation and dividend payments.
